Brain Hierarchy

Close

The brain is split up into three primary divisions, and there are many subdivisions within these three. The three primary divisions are split based on developmental and functional differences; and are the forebrain, the midbrain, and the hindbrain. The cerebral cortex is the outermost layer of the forebrain and is responsible for cognitive functions like thinking, memory, perception, and consciousness, among others. It contains numerous folds to increase surface area, which allows for all the other subdivisions. The cerebral cortex is divided into the left and right hemisphere, and each hemisphere is further divided into the four lobes: frontal, parietal, temporal, and occipital. These lobes are even further divided into many subdivisions, with some notable ones being the hippocampus of the temporal lobe, the prefrontal cortex of the frontal lobe, and the primary visual cortex of the occipital lobe.
